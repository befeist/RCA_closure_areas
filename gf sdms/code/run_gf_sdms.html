Groundfish SDMs in RCAs

Author

Owen Liu

Purpose

Run SDMs and project them to a high-resolution grid within historical RCA footprint, to look at overlap. To produce the data inputs for this script, see sdmTMB-data-construction-glorys-rcas

Example SDM

The final model for each functional group was chosen from a set of eight candidate models. Each sub-model varies in the assumed functional form of the relationship between predictors and CPUE: models represent the relationship of CPUE with bottom temperature and oxygen as either linear or as a GAM spline. The general model formula, therefore, is:

cpue ~ +bottom_temperature + I(bottom_temperature^2) + bottom_oxygen +I(bottom_oxygen^2)

For the “linear” (non-spline) environmental relationships, and:

cpue~ +s(bottom_temperature, k = 3) + s(bottom_oxygen,k=3)

For the spline relationships. The k=3 parameter denotes the maximum allowable “smoothness” of the fitted spline relationship. This spline k parameter is set at 3 for all models.

Furthermore, the models can include spatial random fields, or not, and can include a quadratic depth effort, or not; this gives us the eight candidate model (turning on or off environmental splines, latent spatial fields, and depth effects). Without spatial random fields, the models reduce to simple GLMs or GAMs. Models were fit with a Tweedie link.

For each model, the data were split into model training (80 percent of the data) and testing (20 percent) sets. After checking for successful convergence, the chosen model is the one with the greatest log-likelihood through cross-validation.

Mean Predictions

Maps

Here’s what the predictions of each look like. NOTE: simply for visualization purposes, the color ramps are truncated at -5 on the lower end (i.e., -5 log CPUE units, which equates to 0.007 kg/km2 and functionally absent)

# using raster template, map
x1 <- make_map_rca(pred_fine); x2<-make_map_glorys(pred_coarse)
